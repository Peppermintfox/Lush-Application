Danny Alexander — Future Lush Merchandiser

I have always loved working for you – a company with ethics and products I believe in. However, I have two other loves. They took me to the edge of Cornwall to study illustration and then off around the world. I re-joined the Portsmouth team but have always wanted an opportunity to combine the passion I have for art, travel and natural products. When I saw the advert for a visual merchandiser to bring the Lush brand to Dubai, I knew this was my opportunity to shine. And so, with a little Lush calligraphy of my own, this is my story…

Becoming an Illustrator

From the Cornish countryside of Falmouth University I earned my BA(hons) in Illustration. I worked with a variety of topics, materials and deadlines from 2 months to 24 hours. Discipline came from setting self-initiated ventures and illustrating everything from informative projects, children’s books, adult fiction and advertising campaigns. By experimenting and being open to new techniques I became very versatile and adapt easily to suit any project.

Managing Creative Champions

I curated a fundraiser for a group of illustrators to publish their work. The show was called Falphabet. Gathering a team of 26 artists, I assigned briefs, deadlines and artwork specifications. By directing the group effectively and negotiating terms for an exhibition with a local shop owner, I was able to bring the vision to life. The show was a success. And it gave me invaluable experience leading a team of talented people.

Setting Out

Seeking new adventures, I set off in 2015 to explore Asia. I visited Vietnam, Cambodia, Thailand and finally headed to Australia for a while. I am an incredibly organised person, so budgeting and arranging flights, accommodation and excursions came naturally to me. But I also learnt to adapt and balance this with spontaneity. Traveling gave me independence and helped me prove to myself I was capable of dealing calmly with new and unknown situations.

Settling In

Respecting local culture is hugely important to me. I make an effort to research a place and learn some of the language and key phrases as part of my travel preparation; even if I have to print them on a piece of paper and gently explain pork is not a vegetarian substitute. That's just one of the things that helps me to settle easily into any new country I visit.

A Workplace To Be Proud Of

My career at Lush began as a Christmas Temp in 2012. I loved my job and came back to the Portsmouth store in 2016 after my travels. My colleagues are like family to me and I am proud to work for a company making the world a better place. I can’t sell what I don’t believe in and our practices and charitable work are some of my favourite things about the company.

Learning The Brand

Portsmouth is a brilliant store with strong visual merchandising. My manager and his team communicate a clear vision and have taught me about the brand, company standards and how to tackle those tricky gift walls! I’ve already learnt a great deal from training sessions, assisting on merchandising nights and new window displays. As a creative person I can put my natural eye for detail, colour and composition into these areas and am keen to continue developing in this field.

My Cup Of Tea

(Earl Grey, incase you were wondering.)

I know how rare it is to find an opportunity like this. I hope that my story explains why this role is my cup of tea and shows you a real life example of how I can think outside the box and go the extra mile to deliver branded projects within a tight deadline.

Connecting The Dots

In summary, I shared these chapters with you to demonstrate three things:

  1. I know — from my studies and experience — the power and practise of visual communication and can manage team-based creative projects effectively.

  2. I understand first-hand what it means to live in new countries and adapt quickly to different cultures.

  3. I care about the company and connect personally with the brand. With a little investment, I know I have the potential to do great work.

If these are traits you value in a candidate for this role I hope you might consider me for an interview.
